The System

How the Full Platform Works

The sensing layer monitors every worker in real time. It determines the precise moment each worker's heat strain requires a break and directs them to a shared cooling station, maximizing the cooling ROI from each station and minimizing station count required on site.

  • MON
    Monitoring Layer Continuous, per-worker tracking of heat strain, combining physiological signals and workload to estimate when each person needs a break.
  • COOL
    Shared Cooling Stations Mains-powered stations with multiple cooling positions. Workers receive rapid, targeted cooling during optimally timed breaks, far exceeding the impact of wearables alone.
  • AI
    AI Queue Optimization & Station Routing An AI scheduling engine balances cooling urgency across workers in real time, minimizes wait times, and routes the highest-strain workers to stations first, maximizing cooling ROI per station.
  • ML
    AI-Driven Predictive Heat Models Machine learning models trained on each worker's biometric history forecast heat strain and required station count for upcoming weather, allowing employers to right-size cooling infrastructure to actual demand.

Temperature Reduction per Session

Deployed pilots are designed to target 0.3–1.0°C core temperature reduction per 10–15 minute cooling break, with corresponding heart-rate reductions, relative to uncooled rest. These figures are directional, based on published data for localized high-capacity cooling and internal modeling, and will be validated in field deployments.
